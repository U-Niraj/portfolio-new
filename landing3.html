Computer Aided Cutting

Computer-aided cutting (CAC) is all about using computer-controlled machines to precisely cut materials. This technology allows for the creation of detailed designs across a range of materials like metal, wood, and plastic. One of its key benefits is efficiencyâ€”it speeds up production, saves on labor costs, and minimizes material waste. This not only makes manufacturing processes more sustainable but also ensures high-quality end products.

Objectives

We were tasked with forming groups of four to improve task efficiency and collaboration. This setup promoted focused teamwork, clear communication, and effective task distribution. Emphasizing individual work documentation through videos and images helped track progress and showcase each team member's contributions.

Day 1

On day 1, we dove into learning LaserCAD software, delving into its functionalities and capabilities for laser cutting and engraving. Additionally, we formed groups to tackle tasks collaboratively, leveraging each member's skills and expertise to enhance learning and task completion efficiency. This hands-on experience with LaserCAD and group collaboration set a solid foundation for our journey into digital fabrication and design.

Day 2

On day 2, we had hands-on experience with the laser cutter, putting our knowledge of LaserCAD into practice. This practical session allowed us to understand the intricacies of laser cutting and engraving, exploring various materials and designs. Working with the laser cutter enhanced our skills and confidence, paving the way for more complex projects and creative exploration in digital fabrication.

Day 3

On day 3, we delved into the technique of press fitting with laser-cut parts. This involved learning how to design parts that fit together snugly without the need for additional adhesives or fasteners. The session focused on precision engineering and ensuring that components interlock seamlessly, expanding our capabilities in creating functional and intricate assemblies using laser cutting technology.

Day 4

On day 4, we delved into the art of engraving, exploring techniques and tools for adding intricate designs and patterns onto various materials. This session expanded our understanding of laser cutting and engraving capabilities, allowing us to create personalized and detailed finishes on our projects.

What we learned

At the conclusion of the computer-aided cutting module, we gained a comprehensive understanding of digital fabrication techniques. We learned to use LaserCAD software for precise designs and engraving, explored press fitting to create seamless assemblies, and honed our skills in laser cutting for intricate shapes and patterns. This module equipped us with the knowledge and practical experience needed to leverage computer-aided cutting technologies effectively in our projects, opening doors to innovative and precise manufacturing processes.
